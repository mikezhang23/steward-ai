Concierge

Saturday morning. Your water heater dies.

8:02 AM
You text Steward
"No hot water. Water heater is done. 50-gallon Rheem in the garage, about 10 years old." You snap two photos.
8:04 AM
Your pricing briefing arrives
"Water heater replacement in 89139 typically runs $900 to $1,400 for a 50-gallon tank. Should include removal, new unit, plumbing connection, and leak test. Expansion tank is a legit add-on at $40 to $80. If anyone suggests re-piping, get a separate quote."
11:00 AM
The contractor arrives
He looks at the unit, checks a few things, and says: "You need a new water heater plus I'd recommend re-routing the gas line. $1,950 total."
You text Steward
"He wants $1,950. Says I need a gas line re-route too."
11:01 AM
Steward coaches you
"The water heater replacement is fair at $1,200 to $1,400. But the gas line re-route was not in the original scope and adds $500+. Ask him why the re-route is necessary. If the existing line passes inspection, you don't need it. Counter at $1,300 for just the water heater and expansion tank."
11:05 AM
You negotiate with confidence
The contractor agrees the gas line can stay. You settle on $1,350 including the expansion tank. You saved $600 and you know you paid a fair price. Total time with Steward: about 4 minutes.
